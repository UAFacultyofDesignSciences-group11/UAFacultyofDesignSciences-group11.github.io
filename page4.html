The concept

Creative Idea
Result

In this case study we place multidisciplinarity in a broad context by taking a closer look at philosophies, work and activities from, among other things,
the art and design world. This allows us to use ideas, material applications, research and working methods and thought patterns that are specific to a (design) discipline usable in our own design practice. We call this a cross-over of creative processes with designs at the interface between disciplines. 

From the research of these artworks we have come to an agreement that we take reflection as the starting point for our artwork. We would like to translate both aspects of reflection, which can be both symbolic and literal, into our visual art. To reflect a literal reflection, we can use materials such as glass, mirrors, water, oil, light, and so on. We then put these reflective materials together to arrive at one compostition, in which fragments of the body are reflected. As a symbolic reflection we map out a problem that is happening in the world today, such as social media, in which people now look at someone faster than at themselves. We want the spectator to "participate" in the artwork by means of self-reflection.
