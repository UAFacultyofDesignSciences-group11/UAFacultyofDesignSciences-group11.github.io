WHO WE ARE

Two Face is an artwork made by a team of four interior architecture students at the University of Antwerp. We were instructed to create a multidisciplinary work of art at scale 1:1. We all have a certain background within architecture or interior. Our background was useful for the realisation of the project as some of us already have some experience in manufactering furniture.

WHAT WE MADE

We made a composition composed of separate reflective elements, each with its own perception or meaning. They are assembled into a visually coherent entirety, and are each attached separately to a construction that forms the focal point of the work. All the reflective objects are connected to the structure with profiles made of aluminum.

 












By attaching the mirrors in different degrees to the wall we have created a curved shape. This gives the visitor the feeling of being surrounded by the mirrors and reflective objects. Because of the curved shape the reflections get an extra dimension.

Detail sketch of the attachment of the mirrors and objects to the OSB wall. These are glued to an aluminum profile that is bent into the correct degrees. Afterwards these are screwed onto the OSB wall.

The result is an assembly of all kinds of mirrors and reflective objects from everyday life. When the visitor stands in front of the art object, he or she will reflect in different ways. The work invites the visitor to look at himself differently.

CASESTUDY

In this casestudy we place multidisciplinarity in a broad context by taking a closer look at philosophies, work and activities from, among other things,
the art and design world. This allows us to use ideas, material applications, research and working methods and thought patterns that are specific to a (design) discipline usable in our own design practice. We call this a cross-over of creative processes with designs at the interface between disciplines. 

CREATIVE IDEA

From the research of different artworks we have come to an agreement that we take reflection as the starting point for our artwork. We would like to translate both aspects of reflection, which can be both symbolic and literal, into our visual art. To reflect a literal reflection, we can use materials such as glass, mirrors, water, oil, light, and so on. We then put these reflective materials together to arrive at one compostition, in which fragments of the body are reflected. As a symbolic reflection we map out a problem that is happening in the world today, such as social media, in which people now look at someone faster than at themselves. We want the spectator to "participate" in the artwork by means of self-reflection.

METHOD

The artwork is a composition that consists of individual reflective objects, each with its own perception or meaning. They are brought together in a visually coherent whole and form the focal point of the work. The whole of reflective objects is connected to the structure by means of aluminum profiles.
