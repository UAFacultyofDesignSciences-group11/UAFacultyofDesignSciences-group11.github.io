CASESTUDY


In this casestudy we place multidisciplinarity in a broad context by taking a closer look at philosophies, work and activities from, among other things,
the art and design world. This allows us to use ideas, material applications, research and working methods and thought patterns that are specific to a (design) discipline usable in our own design practice. We call this a cross-over of creative processes with designs at the interface between disciplines. 

CREATIVE IDEA


Anish Kapoor is a contemporary artist who intrigued us with his works of art, because he focuses on looking. It is remarkable how Kapoor manages to blur the boundaries between reality and imagination and to invite the visitor to look at the artwork in a different, attentive way. In his work Yet Untitled small fragments are shown by means of reflective material, which together form a unique composition.

It is interesting how the artist José Dávila deals with different, often contrasting materials. In his works Practical Structures, Tectonic Games or Doubt and Desire and Die Feder und Elefant he uses large-scale reflective elements, which are kept upright by industrial ratchet bands. As such, he creates great dramatic visual and physical effects of height, weight and balance. 

Dávila draws his inspiration from concepts rooted in minimalism and conceptual art, and encourages the viewer to wonder where the artist's hand begins and ends. The rough, unpolished marble countered by the nylon bands demonstrates the dichotomy between naturally occurring materials and the manufactured ones. In his Cut-outs On going he goes through the 'cutting out' of the central subject in documentary photography, forcing the viewer to imagine that, as an act of creativity, that appeals to the common universal memory. This series of works show a great multidisciplinarity.

We have compiled a bundle in which we discuss, among other things, these intriguing works extensively. We also analyzed some guest lectures by multidisciplinary thinkers, which make us think about combining different techniques, domains or methods. The PDF of this bundle is below. 

These artists inspired us to work on the theme of reflection. That is why we started looking for reflective materials from our daily lives. Below you can see some photos of our selected items for the collection.

METHOD

What’s interesting about our project is that it is made out of found or recycled objects. This ensured that our first assignment in this case study was the collection of all reflective objects that we encountered on our path. The artwork consists of 70% mirrors in different shapes and sizes, the remaining 30% are found objects that can be found everywhere. These are for example silver parts of a service, but also building materials such as aluminum or even technical equipment such as a mobile phone. From this complete collection, we could draw a composition. Everything that followed afterwards was calculating thinking about a matching structure. We chose a wall and floor construction from OSB boards, beams and industrial pallets. On the wall, the collected objects were placed in a horizontally curved shape at eye level.

This was made possible by the use of aluminum flat profiles that were bent into different degrees and were cut to different lengths. The construction of the floor, which is built up of pallets, on which OSB boards are also attached, serves as a platform on which people have to stand at the correct distance from the work of art.

To secure the wall to the pallets, a recess is provided in between which the wall can stand upright. This is additionally secured with tension cables from the floor construction to the top of the wall.

In order to attract as many passersby as possible who can participate in our artwork, we searched for the ideal location to place our object. Not only the audience was important in our choice, also the environment and the light were important criteria. To answer these conditions, we searched for a public space within our school that could put our work of art extra in the spotlight. In the end, we opted for the entrance of the building on the Mutsaard Campus. We were convinced that at this location our artwork would stand out and that there would be an interaction with teachers and fellow students.
